\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The two 7-day runs in the main report are extended to the \textbf{whole of 2025}: the 6-agent
green-hydrogen real-time market is cleared by online FACET on \textbf{362 days} ($3$ DST/data-gap days
excluded), \textbf{$34{,}752$ dispatch steps}, and benchmarked step-for-step against a tuned ADMM
($\rho=0.01$) on the \emph{identical} parameter sequence, warm-start block. Two conclusions. First, the
\emph{communication} claim is robust across every month: FACET clears a step in a \textbf{median of one}
inter-agent round versus ADMM's \textbf{$\sim$67}, winter to summer, spending $1.5\%$ of ADMM's total
traffic. Second, FACET's accuracy is seasonally modulated and the mechanism is physical: the share of
steps reaching the oracle's $10^{-4}$~kW bar tracks the ceiling-binding fraction ($r=-0.87$), which is
itself driven by renewable scarcity ($r=-0.74$). September --- ERCOT's renewable-low month --- is the
annual worst case. The year revises two headline numbers honestly: iteration-free rate
$99.3\%\!\to\!98.9\%$ and FACET reach $80\%\!\to\!76\%$; the two chosen weeks sat on the optimistic side.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setup}
Every 2025 calendar day is run except \texttt{2025-03-09}, \texttt{2025-11-02}, and \texttt{2025-12-04}
(spring-forward $92$-interval, fall-back $100$-interval, and a $95$-interval data gap --- the RTM feed is
not $96$ steps on these). Each day uses a deterministic per-day seed, the full receding-horizon DAM$\to$RTM
closed loop, and the warm-start benchmark block (all methods initialised from the previous step, the
deployment-realistic regime; the cold-start block remains on the two-week sample). The shared stopping
metric is $\lVert x-x_{\mathrm{ref}}\rVert_\infty<10^{-4}$~kW against a Gurobi oracle. The full
FACET-vs-ADMM table over the year is Table~\ref{tab:bench-warm}.

\section{Communication is robust across the year}
The implementation-independent claim is communication. Over $34{,}752$ steps it does not drift: the median
inter-agent round count is $1$ for FACET and $66$--$68$ for ADMM in every season
(Fig.~\ref{fig:rounds}), and median wall time holds at $\sim\!4$~ms vs $\sim\!135$~ms. This is the number
that survives hardware, language, and network assumptions --- it is now backed by $26\times$ the original
sample.

\begin{figure}[H]\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\linewidth]{year_rounds.pdf}
\caption{Median inter-agent rounds per step by meteorological season. FACET is $\approx1$ in every group;
ADMM sits at $66$--$68$. The $1$-vs-$67$ separation is flat year-round.}
\label{fig:rounds}
\end{figure}

\section{Accuracy tracks the binding fraction}
FACET returns a \emph{certified} equilibrium on every step, but only reaches the oracle bar when the shared
PCC ceiling is slack; when the coalition pins the ceiling it returns a certified but \emph{non-variational}
GNE, off the social optimum by a bounded margin. Across the year the daily relationship is nearly linear
(Fig.~\ref{fig:scatter}): $r=-0.87$ between binding fraction and reach. September clusters in the hard
bottom-right.

\begin{figure}[H]\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.66\linewidth]{year_scatter.pdf}
\caption{Daily FACET reach vs.\ ceiling-binding steps per day (one dot per day, $n=362$). As binding rises,
the share of steps resolved to tolerance falls; September highlighted.}
\label{fig:scatter}
\end{figure}

\section{Seasonal shape and its driver}
Binding is not price-driven --- its dominant correlate is renewable availability ($r=-0.74$): when the wind
and solar farms produce less, the electrolyzers lean on grid import to meet their cumulative-H$_2$ floor and
the fleet pushes into the shared ceiling. The monthly shape (Fig.~\ref{fig:binding}) runs from a spring
trough ($\sim\!15$ binding steps/day in Mar/Apr) to a late-summer/fall ridge ($27$--$32$ in Aug/Sep).
September binds most and is hardest for FACET \emph{even though} its mean real-time price (\$31.3) is
identical to April's (\$31.1). Table~\ref{tab:year-seasonal} summarises.

\section{What the year revises --- honestly}
Two numbers move against the two-week sample. The \textbf{iteration-free rate is $98.9\%$} year-round
($397$ ADMM fallbacks / $34{,}752$ steps) versus $99.3\%$ on Apr$+$Jul, with fallbacks concentrated in
fall (SON $169$ of $397$). \textbf{FACET reach is $76.3\%$} full-year versus $80\%$ in the sample. Neither
weakens the contribution: the claim that matters (communication, $1$ vs $67$; speed, $\sim\!33\times$;
$1.5\%$ traffic share) is fully robust, and the accuracy cost is now \emph{characterised} --- ``worst at
the fall renewable-scarcity peak, bounded at all times'' --- rather than represented by a single favourable
window. The complete per-month ledger is Table~\ref{tab:year-monthly}.
